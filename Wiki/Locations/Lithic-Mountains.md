# Lithic Mountains

Unlike anything Back East. Rugged hills gradually grow into jagged peaks where the highest summits hold permanent snow. Weather changes rapidly at all elevations — a dangerous place to get caught out. Winter travel is not recommended, as some have discovered the hard way. Well-hidden riches draw brave and foolhardy settlers alike.

## Features

- Monumental rock faces
- Toothy crags and vertigo-inducing drops
- Swirling fogs
- Unpredictable precipitation
- Hardy flora and fauna
- Shockingly bright colors amidst a panoply of gray
- Rough-hewn cabins in improbable places
- Well-hidden riches
- Rugged inhabitants

## Inhabitants & Culture

Known for self-reliance. Settlements are rare and those who stay must learn to do things on their own. The potential of gold and other precious resources still draws a sizable number of settlers willing to face the indomitable mountain faces.

## Quest Starter

A wagon train was warned about the dangers of crossing the Lithics in winter but was sure it would complete the passage before the first snow. They were wrong. Whom do you know in the party, and why do they mean enough to you that you're willing to save them from their own ignorance?
