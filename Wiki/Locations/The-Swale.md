# The Swale

## Overview

Very little of the land here can be counted on to be land 365 days out of the year. Rivers change course frequently, sudden rains inundate regions, and every body of water is liable to flood its banks. All this water allows for riotous plant growth and powerful humidity that intensifies both summer's stultifying heat and winter's cold. When floodwaters recede, they leave treasures behind — shrimp, fish, and all manner of man-made riches previously thought reclaimed by nature.

## Description

- Water, water everywhere
- Pervasive marine odor
- Copious plant life
- The squelch of mud underfoot
- Thick clouds of insects
- Ramshackle houses straddling water
- Bobbing buoys signaling underwater traps
- Rivers as roads, boats as stagecoaches

## Known Inhabitants

Badlanders here have not merely adapted to their unstable environment but learned to thrive in it. The river and waterways serve as roads; boats replace stagecoaches. Floodwaters are a resource as much as a hazard.
