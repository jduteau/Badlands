# Session 1 — Arriving in Perdition

## Summary

Jake Powell rides into the small town of Perdition after three days on the trail, stables his horse Wrangler at Miller's Livery, and settles in for the night at the Dusty Rose saloon. There he meets Sheriff Tom Blaine and Mae Sutter, learns of a murdered ranch hand named Eli Crane and forty stolen cattle at the Harker Ranch, and agrees to investigate for $20. The next morning he rides north toward the ranch, survives a tornado on the open range, and discovers bones in a creek arroyo — bearing the initials E.C. on a hat band. He arrives at the Harker Ranch at session's end and is hired on as a hand by Clara Harker.

---

## Narrative

Three days out from the last town, Jake Powell rides Wrangler into Perdition as the sun dips behind the canyon buttes. The town is small — a main street, a saloon, a livery, a boarding house — and it watches him with the flat eyes of a place that has learned to measure strangers.

He stables Wrangler at Miller's Livery for a dollar — full treatment, gear locked in the tack room. Miller volunteers that Sheriff Blaine has been buying strangers drinks and asking questions about the road north, a warning delivered as an afterthought.

At the Dusty Rose, Mae Sutter pours him honest whiskey and Sheriff Tom Blaine drifts over with a second shot on the house. Blaine is tired, watchful, and short of answers: a hand named Eli Crane was found throat-cut in a gulch east of the Harker Ranch, forty head of prime cattle gone with him. Blaine and Old Man Harker have history, enough that the sheriff can't ride up there without a fight. He needs someone with no stake in old grudges.

Mae fills in the human side of it: Eli Crane, twenty-two or twenty-three, harmless, bought a round for the house the last time he won at cards. She suggests Jake talk to Clara Harker — the old man's daughter — rather than Harker himself. Jake lingers at the bar, slides over to sit beside Blaine, and offers his services. He has done some sleuthing in his past. Blaine accepts, offers $20 for something solid — a name, a direction, proof — and shakes his hand. "Powell," says Jake. Blaine files it away.

Jake pays for the stew, checks into Mother Aguilar's boarding house, and sleeps the best sleep he's had in weeks. Morning: coffee, eggs, beans, ham, biscuits. Then Miller's to collect Wrangler and head north-northeast per Blaine's hand-drawn map.

The ride turns ugly by midday. A tornado drops from a bruise-colored sky, strikes the open range, and throws Wrangler when she rears. Jake hits the ground hard — a branch or fencepost catches his shoulder — but he gathers himself, finds Wrangler uninjured, and makes camp in a creek-side overhang as night and rain close in.

It is there, under the overhanging bank, that the flood-swelled creek exposes bones. Human-sized. Half-buried in silt. A silver belt buckle — longhorn steer design, quality work, not cheap — lies beside the ribcage. A rotting hat band is tangled in the roots above the skull. Jake wades out in the cold water and retrieves both. In the moonlight he makes out letters scratched inside the band:

**E. C.**

Eli Crane — according to Blaine — is buried in Perdition's cemetery, found dead in a gulch east of the ranch. These bones are in an arroyo a day's ride away. Jake holds two objects that don't fit the story he was given and pockets them.

He sleeps in fits, wakes to washed-clean country and a pale sun, and rides on to the Harker Ranch. Clara Harker meets him on the porch with a rifle and steady eyes. He mentions Mae Sutter. She hires him on for a week — a dollar a day, meals, the tack room bunk — and sends him to settle in while she deals with her ailing father inside. In the bunkhouse, exploring before lunch, Jake finds the empty bunk in the corner with **E.C.** carved into the frame. Footsteps approach outside as the session ends.
