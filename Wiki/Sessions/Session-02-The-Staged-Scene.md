# Session 2 — The Staged Scene

## Summary

Jake Powell's first full day at Harker Ranch opens with a confrontation with [[Wiki/NPCs/Old-Man-Harker]], who reveals he needs someone to investigate [[Wiki/NPCs/Eli-Crane]]'s murder independently of [[Wiki/NPCs/Tom-Blaine]]. Jake strikes a deal: report to Harker first, but he won't lie to Blaine if asked directly. After lunch with Clara and her father, Jake rides to the north pasture crime scene and discovers the official account was staged — Eli was killed upstream at a boulder cluster and his body moved to the creek bed. A town shoe print and a worn military brass button (eagle clutching arrows) indicate the killer is no local rancher. Jake returns at dusk to find the other hands, [[Wiki/NPCs/Samuel-Reeves]] and [[Wiki/NPCs/Hector-Garza]], have come in from the range.

---

## Narrative

Jake Powell's first full day at Harker Ranch began with an unexpected confrontation. Old Man Harker—frail but fierce—discovered Jake in the bunkhouse near Eli Crane's old bunk and demanded to know who he was. After Jake explained Clara had hired him, the old man revealed his true need: someone to investigate Eli's murder, since Sheriff Blaine is barred from the property. Jake struck a deal—he'd report to Harker first, but wouldn't lie to Blaine if asked directly.

At lunch in the main house, Jake met [[Wiki/NPCs/Maria]] the cook and sat with Clara and her father. Clara confirmed the ranch's troubles—scattered cattle, spooked hands, and Eli's death—and made clear she runs operations while her father remains owner in name. She warned Jake that whatever's happening isn't finished and asked him to report anything strange to her.

With permission to explore the land, Jake rode Wrangler north to the creek bed where Eli was supposedly found. His careful investigation revealed the official story was wrong. A blood stain on a rock at waist height showed Eli was standing—or held—when first struck. Faint drag marks led upstream to a boulder cluster where a larger blood pool marked the true death site. Someone had staged the body back at the creek.

More disturbing: a preserved boot print behind a cottonwood showed a flat, hard-edged sole—a town shoe, not ranch wear. Beside it, half-buried in dirt, Jake found a brass military button bearing an eagle clutching arrows, worn almost smooth with age.

Jake returned to the ranch at sunset. Samuel Reeves and Hector Garza had come in from the range, their voices carrying from the bunkhouse. Old Man Harker sat watching from the porch, Winchester across his lap, waiting for a report Jake couldn't safely give in the open.
