# Session 3 — The Imposter Revealed

## Summary

Jake Powell returned from the crime scene, met [[Wiki/NPCs/Samuel-Reeves]] and [[Wiki/NPCs/Hector-Garza]] in the bunkhouse, attended a quiet supper with the Harker household, then held a secret midnight meeting with [[Wiki/NPCs/Old-Man-Harker]] in the smokehouse. Jake revealed his findings: the body was staged, a worn cavalry button was found at the true kill site, and bones with an E.C. hat band were found in an arroyo a day's ride away — meaning the real Eli Crane died long ago and an imposter had been working the ranch for months. Harker provided Jake with $40 and a cover story as supply runner to investigate in Perdition.

---

## Narrative

As dusk settled over Harker Ranch, Jake Powell rode in from the north pasture carrying evidence that would shatter everything the Harkers believed about Eli Crane's death. He acknowledged Old Man Harker on the porch with a subtle nod but wisely avoided drawing attention, instead heading to the bunkhouse to meet the remaining hands.

Samuel Reeves and Hector Garza received him with the wary hospitality of men who'd seen too many hands come and go since the troubles began. Over conversation, Jake learned that Eli had been a young hand, maybe twenty-two or twenty-three, who'd worked the spread for six years and had a gentle way with horses. The men spoke of him with genuine grief, and of the shadow that had fallen over the ranch since his death.

Supper at the main house was a quiet affair. Clara Harker maintained her composed authority while Old Man Harker barely touched his pozole, his illness weighing heavy. Maria served in silence. The mundane talk of fence repairs and work assignments masked the tension Jake could feel from the old man's knowing glances.

After the house went dark, Jake made his way to the smokehouse where Harker waited with a cigarette glowing in the shadows. There, in candlelight, Jake revealed the truth: the body had been dragged to the creek bed and staged, and a worn cavalry button from the Indian Wars era was found at the actual kill site — a boulder with sight lines in three directions, a place for secret meetings.

But the greater revelation shook Harker to his core. Jake produced the silver belt buckle and described the bones he'd found in an arroyo a day's ride away, with a hat band bearing the initials E.C. The real Eli Crane had been dead long enough to be bones — which meant the man they'd buried, the man who'd worked the ranch these past months, was an imposter.

Harker's mind raced through possibilities: water rights, smuggler trails through the hills, old mines and caves on the property. Someone had planted a man on Harker land to learn its secrets, and when that man had served his purpose, his handler — likely the cavalry man who'd lost that button — had eliminated him.

To give Jake cover for investigating in town, Harker provided a list of supplies and forty dollars, establishing him as the ranch's new errand runner. The old man's final words carried the weight of a man who knew his time was short: find out who took the real Eli from them, and when you do, come tell him first. What happens after would be his business.

Jake returned to the tack room with secrets heavy in his pocket and Harker's warning echoing in his mind — whoever did this was patient, methodical, and probably still watching from somewhere in Perdition.
