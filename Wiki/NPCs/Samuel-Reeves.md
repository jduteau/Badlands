# Samuel Reeves

Ranch hand at [[Wiki/Locations/Harker-Ranch]]. One of two hands who remained after Eli Crane's death and the exodus of other workers. Returned from the range at the end of Session 2.

---

## Description

Broad-shouldered, sun-dark, maybe thirty. Working man's hands — calloused, honest. Voice comes out flat and road-weary. Sits on the edge of his bunk and works boots with an awl at the end of a long day. His grip is firm. He goes quiet and careful around the subject of Eli Crane. [Session 3]

---

## Role

One of the two surviving ranch hands at the Harker spread. He and [[Wiki/NPCs/Hector-Garza]] have been working the range during a period of fear and instability that drove others off. Whether he knows anything about Eli Crane's murder is unknown.

---

## PC Relationship

**[Session 2]** Heard but not met — voices carrying from the bunkhouse at dusk when Jake returned from the crime scene.

**[Session 3]** Formally met in the bunkhouse doorway at dusk. Guarded but fair — introduced himself, shook hands. Got briefly sharp when Jake asked about Eli's role (misread it as digging for gossip), then softened. Explained how he and Garza found Eli by the line shack and how half the hands left that night. Told Jake to keep his head down. Clara assigned them fence work together at dawn.

---

## What the PC Knows

- Ranch hand at Harker Ranch; one of only two remaining; going on three years at the spread
- Broad-shouldered, about thirty; working man through and through
- Found Eli by the old line shack; half the hands packed up that same night
- Stayed because Clara is good to them — and Thomas when he's lucid
- Genuinely grieved over Eli; calls him a good man who had a gentle way with horses
- Warned Jake to keep his head down and let sleeping dogs lie about anything besides the work
