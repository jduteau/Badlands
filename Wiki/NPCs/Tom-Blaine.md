# Tom Blaine

Sheriff of [[Wiki/Locations/Perdition]]. Late thirties. Dark mustache. The kind of stillness that comes from patience or exhaustion — possibly both.

---

## Description

A lean man with a tin star on his chest and tired eyes. He sits alone at the far end of the Dusty Rose bar and nurses a whiskey with the air of someone who's going to be there a while. His grip is firm and calloused. He chooses his words carefully and means what he says about paying his debts.

---

## Role

The local law for Perdition and the surrounding territory. One man covering too much ground. He is nominally investigating the murder of [[Wiki/NPCs/Eli-Crane]] and the Harker cattle theft but is effectively blocked: [[Wiki/NPCs/Old-Man-Harker]] won't have him on the spread.

---

## PC Relationship

**[Session 1]** Bought Jake a drink, gave him Blaine's map to the Harker Ranch, and hired him as an informal investigator for $20 — payable on delivery of "something solid: a name, a direction, proof." Shook hands. Gave a meaningful warning about how Harker would react to a stranger, and suggested Jake present himself as looking for work.

---

## What the PC Knows

- Sheriff of Perdition
- Investigating Eli Crane's murder and the Harker cattle theft
- Has unresolved history with Old Man Harker; Harker won't allow Blaine on his land
- Usually at the jail mornings; the Dusty Rose evenings
- "Pays his debts" — Jake has his word of $20 and a standing favor
- Served in the Army before becoming sheriff — established by [[Wiki/NPCs/Old-Man-Harker]] in Session 3 (though Harker doubts Blaine is cold enough to be the killer) [Session 3]

---

## GM Truth

A decent lawman doing his job under difficult circumstances. His conflict with Harker predates this investigation and complicates it. Whether there is more to his relationship with Harker is TBD.

No bond held. An informal working relationship established.
