# Old Man Harker

Owner of [[Wiki/Locations/Harker-Ranch]]. Ill. Suspicious. Has a long-standing conflict with [[Wiki/NPCs/Tom-Blaine]] severe enough that Blaine cannot set foot on Harker land without a fight.

---

## Description

Fifty or more years old. Weathered face and sun-faded clothes. Lean, stooped, with a hitch in the breathing you can hear across a room. Pale eyes that stay sharp despite the failing body. Leans on a gnarled walking stick. Still capable of blocking a doorway by force of will alone. The sickness is real — the coughing is bad — but the hardness in his gaze is older and more durable than any illness. [Session 2]

---

## Role

The ranch owner. His illness has left Clara to run the operation. His conflict with Blaine is the political complication that required Blaine to hire an outside investigator. He took Eli Crane's death hard — possibly because Eli was the same age as Clara.

---

## PC Relationship

**[Session 1]** Not directly met. Heard only — voice from inside the house. Jake is established on the ranch as a hired hand.

**[Session 2]** Directly encountered. Harker confronted Jake in the bunkhouse, tested him hard, then hired him privately to investigate Eli Crane's murder — deal struck: Jake reports to Harker first, won't lie to Blaine if asked directly. Harker accepted Jake's terms ("You got sand, I'll give you that"). Gave Jake leave to investigate the north pasture. Sat on the porch at dusk with Winchester waiting for Jake's return.

**[Session 3]** Met Jake secretly in the smokehouse at midnight. Received the full report: body staged, cavalry button at the true kill site, bones with E.C. initials in the arroyo. Was shaken by the imposter revelation — confirmed the grave body had severe facial damage from animals and sun; he had not let Clara view it. Concluded: an imposter was planted on his land to map water rights, smuggler trails, and unknown resources. Gave Jake a supply-run cover story ($40, twice-weekly errands to Perdition). Signal agreed: lantern in main house window = safe to meet after midnight. Named suspects: unnamed spring surveyor, Blaine (Army background), [[Wiki/NPCs/Doc-Whitmore]] (patched soldiers). Warned Jake the killer is patient and probably still in Perdition. Asked Jake to find who took the real Eli and report to him first — "What happens after is my business."

## What the PC Knows

- Owner of the Harker Ranch; ill; Clara manages day-to-day
- Deep antagonism toward Sheriff Blaine — Blaine is formally barred from Harker land
- Took Eli Crane's death hard — Eli worked there six years, joined at sixteen
- He believes Blaine's "rustlers" theory is convenient nonsense; thinks someone wanted them looking for cattle instead of something else
- He suspects the cattle scatter was a deliberate distraction
- He suspects Blaine's interest is in protecting his own record, not finding the killer
- He respects a man who doesn't lie to him; accepted Jake's counter-terms without blinking [Session 2]
- The real Eli Crane came to the ranch at sixteen, six years ago; Harker's illness had kept him off the pastures for months — he admits he stopped really looking at his hands [Session 3]
- The imposter was almost certainly sent to surveil and map Harker land — water rights from the north pasture creek (feeds Perdition's aquifer), old Apache and smuggler trails in the hills, unknown mine shafts and caves [Session 3]
- The killer (the imposter's handler) is patient and methodical; would stay in Perdition and watch rather than run [Session 3]
- Suspects with military background in or near Perdition: unnamed spring surveyor, [[Wiki/NPCs/Tom-Blaine]] (Army — though Harker doubts he's cold enough for this), [[Wiki/NPCs/Doc-Whitmore]] (patched soldiers) [Session 3]
- Wants Jake to find who took the real Eli and report to him first; "what happens after is my business" [Session 3]

## History

**[Session 1]** Mentioned by Blaine, Mae Sutter, and Clara Harker. Heard but not seen at the ranch.

**[Session 2]** Confronted Jake in the bunkhouse. Tested him about his connection to Blaine and the law. Hired Jake privately to investigate Eli's murder. Struck a deal on Jake's terms. At dusk: sitting on the porch, Winchester across his lap, watching the yard. Waiting for the report that couldn't safely be given in the open.

**[Session 3]** Met Jake in the smokehouse at midnight. Received the staged-body revelation calmly, then was shaken to his core by the imposter revelation. Admitted the grave body had severe facial damage and he'd kept Clara from viewing it. Spent the meeting working through who might have wanted a planted man on his land and why. Established the supply-run cover story for Jake; agreed on lantern-and-smokehouse communication protocol. Warned Jake the killer is watching and told him not to go anywhere alone at night. Gave Jake $40 and his trust — and a final charge: find who took the real Eli and come to him first.
